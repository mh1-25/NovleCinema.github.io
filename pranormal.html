Summary

   Rifaat Ismail,sixty-six years,Professor of Hematology (formerly) with a fair 
number of universities in Egypt, Europe, and America. I lived a busy life in 
which I searched everywhere for the myths of life that we inherited from our 
ancestors, and they terrified us as well as they terrified them. I faced Count 
Dracula and zombies, entered Dr. Frankenstein’s castle, was subjected to the 
curse of the Pharaohs, played with tarot cards, and much more. Before the 
deadline came or atherosclerosis destroyed my memory,I chose to write my
stories so that young people would know what a fighter against 
superstitions and nonsense I was in my life.

ِabout ahmed Khalid Tawfiq

    Ahmed Khaled Tawfiq (June 10, 1962 - April 2, 2018), 
Egyptian university professor, doctor, writer, author, 
and translator. He is considered the first Arab writer 
in the field of horror literature. It is most famous in 
the field of youth literature, fantasy, and science 
fiction. 
He was nicknamed “The Godfather.” His literary journey 
began with writing the Paranormal series, and although 
horror literature was not prevalent at that time, the 
series achieved great success and was well received by 
the audience. What encouraged him to complete it, after 
which he released the Fantasia series in 1995, and the 
Safari series in 1996.
