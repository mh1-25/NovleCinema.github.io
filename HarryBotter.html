Author

:

J. K. Rowling



J.K. Rowling is the renowned author of the Harry Potter series, born Joanne Rowling in 1965. She introduced readers to the magical world of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry in 1997 with the release of "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone." The series captivated audiences globally, making Rowling one of the most successful authors of all time. Beyond Harry Potter, she has penned novels for adults under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith. Rowling is also known for her philanthropy and activism, supporting various charitable causes and advocating for social issues. Her creativity and dedication have earned her widespread admiration, solidifying her as one of the most influential authors of the 21st century.

Summary Film

The film "Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone" follows the story of Harry Potter, who discovers on his eleventh birthday that he is a wizard and receives an invitation to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. At Hogwarts, Harry befriends new companions like Ron and Hermione and uncovers secrets about his family and past. Harry and his friends embark on a quest to find the Philosopher's Stone, believed to grant immortality to its possessor. Along the way, Harry faces numerous challenges and adventures with the help of his friends and Hogwarts professors. He ultimately succeeds in defeating the villain who seeks the Philosopher's Stone for nefarious purposes. The story concludes with Harry and his friends successfully protecting the stone and restoring peace to Hogwarts.
